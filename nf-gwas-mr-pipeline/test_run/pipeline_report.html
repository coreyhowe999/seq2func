Project Summary

Background

Genome-wide association studies (GWAS) have identified thousands of genetic variants associated with complex diseases and traits, yet the vast majority of these associations are correlative rather than causal. Distinguishing true causal relationships from confounded associations is critical for drug target identification and validation, as investing in a non-causal target can lead to costly late-stage clinical trial failures. Mendelian Randomization (MR) addresses this challenge by using genetic variants as instrumental variables—leveraging the random assortment of alleles at conception to approximate a natural randomized controlled trial. When combined with rigorous GWAS summary statistics quality control and polygenic risk score (PRS) modelling, MR provides a powerful framework to prioritize genetically supported drug targets and estimate the likely direction and magnitude of therapeutic intervention. This pipeline implements a complete post-GWAS analysis workflow, from raw summary statistics through causal inference, designed to support the kind of human-genetics-driven drug discovery conducted at organizations like 5 Prime Sciences.

Methods

Synthetic GWAS summary statistics were generated for an exposure trait (simulating a protein quantitative trait locus study) and an outcome trait (simulating coronary artery disease) across up to 22 chromosomes, with realistic allele frequency distributions, imputation quality scores, and planted causal, exposure-only, and pleiotropic signals. Quality control was performed per chromosome using Polars lazy evaluation and included removal of variants with minor allele frequency <0.01, imputation INFO score <0.8, palindromic allele pairs (A/T, C/G), extreme effect sizes (|β| > 10), low sample size, and duplicate rsIDs. Genomic inflation (λGC) was computed at each stage. Independent lead variants were identified via greedy distance-based LD clumping (window = 10,000 kb, P < 5×10-8). Two-sample MR was conducted using four methods implemented from first principles: the Wald ratio for the single strongest instrument, inverse-variance weighted (IVW) fixed-effects meta-analysis, MR-Egger regression (testing for directional pleiotropy via the intercept), and the weighted median estimator (robust to up to 50% invalid instruments, with bootstrap standard errors). Instrument strength was evaluated with the F-statistic, and heterogeneity was assessed with Cochran's Q and I2. In parallel, polygenic risk scores were calculated at eight p-value thresholds (5×10-8 to 1.0) for 1,000 simulated individuals, with predictive accuracy measured as R2 against a simulated phenotype.

Results

After quality control, 37,328 of 100,000 input variants were retained across all chromosomes. LD clumping identified 5 independent lead loci reaching genome-wide significance. These loci were carried forward as genetic instruments into the MR analysis. Instrument strength was adequate (mean F-statistic = 44.0), indicating low risk of weak-instrument bias. The IVW estimate of the causal effect of the exposure on the outcome was β = 0.0049, SE = 0.0674, P = 9.42e-01, with the MR-Egger intercept at 0.0115 (P = 1.84e-01), consistent with minimal directional pleiotropy. Heterogeneity across instruments was low (I2 = 0.0%; Cochran's Q P = 6.93e-01), suggesting the instruments provide consistent evidence. Polygenic risk score analysis identified an optimal p-value threshold of 1e+00 (R2 = 0.3161), and the PRS distribution showed clear separation between simulated cases and controls, confirming that the planted genetic architecture is recoverable. Workflow

The pipeline is implemented in Nextflow DSL2, which separates each analytical step into modular processes connected by typed channels. The workflow exploits a scatter-gather parallelism pattern: synthetic data generation and QC execute independently for each chromosome (scatter), results are collected genome-wide (gather), and LD clumping operates on the merged dataset. The pipeline then forks into two concurrent branches—Mendelian Randomization and Polygenic Risk Score calculation—before a final gather step assembles all outputs into this HTML report.
